# Best Festival Luggage & Bags 2026: From Carry-Ons to Camelbaks

The five bags you actually need — and nothing else. Getting your bag system right before you leave is the difference between a smooth festival experience and a chaotic one.

---

## 1. Cabin Carry-On Suitcase — Getting There

If you're flying, a hard-shell carry-on that fits Ryanair/easyJet overhead bins saves £40+ in checked bag fees. Look for a 20-inch spinner with TSA-approved locks. Non-negotiable for budget European festival flights.

> Ryanair's max: 40x20x25cm under seat, 55x40x20cm overhead (with Plus/Priority).

## 2. 65L Hiking & Festival Rucksack — Camping

The main pack for camping festivals. 65L fits tent, sleeping bag, wellies, clothes and food. Pick one with a waterproof base, external attachment points for boots, and padded hip straps — you'll be walking 2km from the car park.

> Pack your sleeping bag at the bottom, tent poles down one side, and heaviest items close to your back.

## 3. Festival Bumbag / Crossbody — On-Site Daily

Your daily carry. Fits phone, cards, cash, earbuds, lip balm and a small power bank. The best festival bumbags are water-resistant, have anti-theft zip placement, and can be worn across the chest as well as the hip.

> Never put your phone in your back pocket at a festival. Front-worn bumbag only.

## 4. Camelbak Hydration Pack — Festival Hydration

For long sets at outdoor stages, a 2L hydration pack keeps you dancing without queuing for water. The Camelbak Classic is the gold standard — also doubles as a small day pack with a 6L dry storage section.

> Fill up at water points every 2–3 hours. Most UK festivals have free refill stations.

10L or 20L dry bag to protect sleeping bag, electronics, and clothes from UK festival rain. Rolls closed at the top, floats if you drop it in a puddle, and costs almost nothing. Pack one even if the forecast looks good.
