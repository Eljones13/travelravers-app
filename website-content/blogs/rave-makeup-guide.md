# How to Do Rave Makeup That Lasts 3 Days: The Ultimate Festival Guide

The complete festival makeup guide for surviving sweat, rain, mosh pits, and three days without a mirror. These products and techniques are used by regular festival ravers — not beauty influencers who've never been to a field.

---

## The 5-Step Festival Makeup System

### Step 1: Primer

Start with a silicone-based primer across your whole face. This creates a barrier between your skin and whatever gets thrown at it — sweat, rain, glitter, dust. Don't skip this step. It's what makes everything else last.

### Step 2: UV / Neon Base

Apply your UV neon base colours where you want them to glow under blacklights. Use a fluffy brush for blending and a fine brush for sharp lines. Lighter skin tones pick up UV colour more easily — go heavier on application if you have a deeper skin tone.

**[Shop UV Neon Face Paint on Amazon UK →](https://www.amazon.co.uk/s?k=uv+neon+face+paint+festival&tag=travelravers-21)**

### Step 3: Glitter

Use cosmetic-grade glitter mixed with a chunky glitter gel or a dedicated glitter primer. Apply with your fingers for maximum coverage. Focus on cheekbones, temples, and décolletage. Never use craft glitter on skin — it's not cosmetic grade and can cause irritation.

**[Shop Cosmetic Body Glitter on Amazon UK →](https://www.amazon.co.uk/s?k=cosmetic+body+glitter+festival&tag=travelravers-21)**

### Step 4: Setting Spray

Douse your face in a heavy-duty setting spray — not a standard one. Look for phrases like "16-hour hold" or "waterproof lock". Do two passes: one with eyes closed, one with eyes open. Let it dry fully before you touch your face again.

**[Shop Makeup Setting Spray on Amazon UK →](https://www.amazon.co.uk/s?k=makeup+setting+spray+long+lasting&tag=travelravers-21)**

### Step 5: Touch-Up Kit

Pack a small zip bag with your setting spray, a travel UV paint, glitter gel, and a mirror. Festival toilets are grim but a 90-second touch-up at the mirror on your phone camera works fine.
